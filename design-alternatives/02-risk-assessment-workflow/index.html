Assumptions & transparency

This panel says plainly what is real, what is synthetic, and what we are still asking FEMA about.

What this demo is

A design concept for running the Preliminary Risk Assessment (PRA) — the 10-question, per-program risk screen required for federal payment-integrity compliance (Payment Integrity Information Act, PIIA) — as managed case work: one case per program per fiscal year, with ownership, lifecycle stages, hard sign-off gates and a complete decision log.

Working assumptions (plain language)

  • The 10-question instrument shown here is an illustrative placeholder. FEMA's real PRA form will replace it; the workflow does not depend on the exact questions.
  • The Preparer / Reviewer role split is our proposal. FEMA has not yet confirmed who prepares and who signs; the role switcher in the header is simulated.
  • Case owners, timestamps, due dates and prior-year assessment records are synthesized for the demo. Real assessment records exist at FEMA from 2018–19 onward and would be imported.
  • A comprehensive assessment is assumed due at least once every 3 years per program; the "comprehensive due" chips are computed from a simulated last-comprehensive year.
  • Fiscal-year spend enters as a file extract from the financial system (WebFMIS, currently modernizing). This demo treats the extract as already received; the ingestion is simulated.
  • Sub-groupings (e.g., debris removal, individual disaster declarations) are never presented as programs — they roll up to the five reportable programs.

Open questions for FEMA

  • Who holds sign-off authority for a PRA, and can preparer and approver be the same person?
  • What is the official due date for a PRA after fiscal-year close?
  • Does the 20% year-over-year (YoY) trigger threshold differ by program or by measure?
  • How should exception-queue dollars be treated in official totals before mapping is confirmed? (This demo excludes them and flags the exclusion.)
  • What evidence must be retained with a signed PRA for Inspector General review?

How the automated mapping earns trust

Illustrative exhibit, precomputed: in a holdout-year grading exercise, the mapping engine auto-mapped 47 of 47 codes correctly, routed 10 low-confidence codes to human review, and made 0 incorrect assignments. The grading answer key is never read by the inference itself. Any code below 0.85 confidence is routed to the exception queue for a human decision — automation proposes, people decide.

Where "AI" appears

Every AI-flavored output here (mapping suggestions, confidence scores, pre-filled answers) is simulated and deterministic — precomputed for the demo. AI never owns a reportable number: the deterministic calculation engine computes every total live from the embedded transactions, and nothing becomes official without a named human sign-off.

Data provenance

SYNTHETIC-DEMO — all spend data is synthetic, calibrated to public obligation envelopes. Program names and disaster declaration (DR) numbers are real public identifiers; TAFS (Treasury Appropriation Fund Symbol) values are clearly-synthetic stand-ins. No live systems are connected; ingestion, roles, records and exports are simulated.
